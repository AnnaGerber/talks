The Reality

"During 2008, the number of things connected to the Internet exceeded the number of people one earth.
By 2020 there will be 50 billion."
—CISCO
We already have an internet of things and it is only going to get bigger: There are already more devices on the internet than people and there have been for the past five years. Admittedly, many of these devices are devices used directly by humans - smartphones, tablets, PCs etc. That's more than 6 times the estimated human population by 2020!

The Internet of Everything

Connecting

  • people: social networks
  • processes: business processes, web services, web apps
  • data: linked data
  • things
What we're heading towards in the near future is a highly connected, highly discoverable network of things, people, processes and data. CISCO uses the term "Internet of Everything" to describe this. I prefer to call it "The Internet". As with people, processes, and data, many of the things connected to the internet will be exposed via the web: continuing the trend of the web shifting from a web of human readable content towards a web of machine processable data and services.

The Open Internet of Everything

many eyes make all bugs shallow

Sometimes referred to as Linus's law - named after Linus Torvalds, founder of Linux.

Internet of Things
Bill of Rights

  • Open is better than closed; this ensures portability between Internet of Things devices.
  • Consumers, not companies, own the data collected by Internet of Things devices
  • Internet of Things devices that collect public data must share that data.
  • Users have the right to keep their data private.
  • Users can delete or back up data collected by Internet of Things devices.
—Limor (Ladyada) Fried, AdaFruit Industries
